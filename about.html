The Canadian Infrastructure Tracking Project

With Canada rolling up its sleeves in an attempt to become more economically self sufficient, infrastructure projects are going to pop up very rapidly. We believe it would be most beneficial to set up a dedicated database for a number of reasons.

Without a proper way to track projects, organization and communication will be subpar leading to either longer wait times for projects to start or finish completion. Another issue is the potential for inefficient spending because there is no current way to tell whether projects are worthwhile and not overlapping.

Using our database will solve these problems. All current infrastructure projects would be inserted with the potential to be queried in numerous ways including by province, status or budget. Communication and allocation of resources become much clearer.

The final and biggest reason to implement this database is that the data can be deployed to a public domain. This provides the opportunity to get Canadians excited and involved with what projects they either want or dislike.
